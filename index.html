Experience in Computer Science

The summer after sophomore year, I interned with SAP, one of the world's largest enterprise software companies. Before starting the program, I only had some basic programming skills. I had used JavaScript to add animations, dialog boxes, and interactivity on websites. So when I started the internship and heard that SAP's software had over millions of lines of code, I could hardly believe it. How was it possible to manage so much code? I found it hard to manage more than a dozen in my own programs. During the internship, I had the chance to see software developers work on different parts of the SAP software. It was interesting to see how people worked on different tasks but kept all their code working together. For example, I learned that code written by one developer had to be reviewed by others before it was implemented into the software. They did this not only to double check that the code really worked, but so that everyone could stay in sync. I used to think of programming as a solitary activity; but in the real world, teamwork and communication are equally important. The internship really sparked my interest in programming and computer science. The rest of the summer, I learned as much about programming as I could, using textbooks and websites like Codeacdemy.com As a result, I decided to take AP Computer Science my Junior year. For the final project, we built a computer lab reservation system. The software displayed a calendar, and users could book time slots with their names and room number. I applied what I had learned during my internship- our team spent a lot of time communicating and outlining the program before we started coding. I subsequently applied my skills to design and build two websites. One website was for a Business Professionals of America (BPA) competition, and the other was a personal blog about my experiences learning classical Indian dance. In my AP Computer Science class, I was disappointed to see that there were very few female students. Being a strong advocate of women in STEM, I am glad to see that the University of Illinois supports aspiring women engineers like me.

Dance

As mentioned above, one of my main interests is dance. I am trained Indian Classical dancer in the dance form Kathak. I started dancing when I was five years old, on Bollywood music. I remember standing in front of the mirror, using my hands and feet, dancing, making expressions, and mimicking what I saw on Hindi television. Seeing my talent, my parents put me into a Bollywood dance school when I six years old. Wanting more of a serious dance experience, I was subsequently enrolled in a Kathak dance school and have grown from there since. I have learned dance for over ten years, and completed my dance graduation in 2015. After graduating, I became a dance teacher in my school, and pursued dance in other capacities, such as starting my own high school dance team, working on my own choreography, and trying to learn new styles of dance. Currently, I am involved in Illini Ishaara, an Indian classical dance team at UIUC. Dance will continue to be a big part of my life both recreationally and maybe even academically. I also have a website I made specifically describing my dance journey. You can check it out at jainshivani.com.
